How to Request an iCloud Activation Lock Removal from Apple (Official Process)

Step 1: Find Your Device’s IMEI or Serial Number

  • You’ll need the IMEI or serial number to identify your device.
  • You can find this on:
    • The original box of your iPhone.
    • Underneath the SIM tray (on some models).
    • Dial *#06# on the phone (if accessible).
    • Via iTunes or Finder on your computer (if connected).
    • If the phone is locked, use Apple’s official guide: How to get IMEI on locked iPhone

Step 2: Prepare Proof of Ownership / Purchase

Apple requires valid proof you legally own the device. This is usually:

  • A receipt or invoice from the original purchase showing:
    • Your name (or the buyer’s name).
    • The device’s IMEI or serial number.
    • Date of purchase.
    • Seller’s information.

If you don’t have the original receipt (e.g., the phone is old or secondhand), you can try:

  • A screenshot from a reliable IMEI checker like https://iunlocker.com/check_icloud.php that shows device info.
  • Any official documentation showing the device is yours.
Note: Apple is strict about this — if they cannot verify ownership, they will deny the unlock request.

Step 3: Go to Apple’s iCloud Unlock Request Form

  • Visit Apple’s official Activation Lock support page:
    https://al-support.apple.com/#/additional-support
  • This form is designed specifically for unlocking Activation Lock on your device.

Step 4: Fill Out the Form

  • Read and accept Apple’s terms.
  • Enter your email address (for correspondence).
  • Enter the IMEI of your locked device.
  • Solve the CAPTCHA.
  • Enter the address where you bought the device (you can enter eBay’s office address if you bought it from eBay).
  • In the section asking “What have you tried so far?”, write what you’ve done to remove Activation Lock (e.g., searched online, no success).

Step 5: Upload Proof of Purchase

  • Upload your proof of purchase or ownership.
  • If you don’t have a formal receipt, upload a screenshot of the IMEI check result (e.g., from https://iunlocker.com/check_icloud.php).
  • Apple uses this to verify you are the legitimate owner.

Step 6: Submit Your Request

  • Review everything carefully.
  • Submit the form.

Step 7: Wait for Apple’s Response

  • Apple will review your request.
  • This may take around 30 hours or sometimes longer.
  • If Apple verifies your proof and ownership, they will remove the Activation Lock remotely.
  • You’ll get an email from Apple confirming the unlock.

Step 8: After Unlock Confirmation

  • Connect your iPhone to Wi-Fi.
  • Restore or activate the iPhone.
  • Activation Lock should now be removed — you can use the phone freely.

Important Notes

  • Only Apple can officially remove Activation Lock.
  • You must provide valid proof of ownership.
  • Apple may deny requests without proper documentation.
  • Do not trust unofficial third-party “unlock” services claiming to bypass Activation Lock.
